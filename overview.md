# Stones

*A digital meditation on Kajiwara's "The Direction of Play"*

---

## Vision

**Stones** is a space between reading and meditation — a minimal, contemplative experience that distills Takeo Kajiwara's Go wisdom into its purest essence.

This is not a book displayed on screen. It is a **digital zen garden** where philosophy, presence, and the ancient game of Go converge.

## Philosophy

### Emptiness Over Accumulation

Like the Zen tradition Kajiwara writes from, this experience values **space over content**, **breath over density**, **presence over information**.

Each chapter becomes a meditation — distilled to core wisdom, presented with room to breathe, accompanied by the stones themselves.

### The Sacred Act of Placement

Every element is intentional:
- The cream background — calm, warm, inviting stillness
- The black enso — imperfect circle, symbol of zen enlightenment
- The table of contents — simple gateway, then disappearing
- The scroll — natural rhythm, leading you through and back

### Simplicity, Robustness, Clarity

We resist complexity. We resist ornamentation. We build only what serves the meditative experience.

## The Experience

**Entry**
You arrive at stillness. A cream page. An enso breathing quietly. A list of chapters.

**Immersion**
You choose. The world falls away. Only text, space, and stones remain. You scroll through wisdom, through game positions, through presence.

**Return**
You reach the end. The chapter fades. You return to the gateway. Choose again, or leave transformed.

## What This Is Not

- Not a comprehensive book display
- Not a game player or teaching tool
- Not busy, cluttered, or information-dense
- Not trying to be clever or impressive

## What This Is

A **quiet space** to sit with Kajiwara's wisdom.

A **beautiful interface** that disappears into the reading.

A **bridge** between ancient philosophy and digital presence.
